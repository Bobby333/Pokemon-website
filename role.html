Pokemon role

The role of the Pokemon means what does that Pokemon do for your team what is their purpose. If you look at a Pokemon team typical teams will have a Pokemon that fulfills a specific task for the team there are several different roles you can chose from for Pokemon that when thinking about a role think about what does your team need and is the Pokemon you want in the role capable of filling it.These roles include:

  • Attacking Lead: A Pokemon in the lead position who attacks immediately and generally does not do any setting up what so ever.
  • Baton Passer: A Pokemon who uses Baton Pass to pass the boosts it accumulates through setup moves to a more suitable teammate.
  • Cleric: A Pokemon who is used to to heal their team members both in health and status effect.
  • Dedicated Lead: A Pokemon used only to start the battle and set up for the battle.
  • Dual Screener: A Pokemon who uses moves to set up defensive barriers, or a heal for other Pokemon on the team.
  • Glass Cannon: A Pokemon who has high offensive power but is very defensively frail.
  • Mixed Sweeper: A sweeper who uses both physical and special offensive moves to do damage.
  • Phazer / Pseudo-hazer / Shuffler: A Pokemon who uses moves that may force the opponent to switch.
  • Pivot (Defensive / Offensive): A pivot is a Pokemon that is generally only used for switching. A defensive pivot is a Pokemon with high defensive or special defensive stats that will slow the opponents momentum while a offensive is a Pokemon with high power that will force the opponent to switch to stop their Pokemon from dying.
  • Physical Sweeper: A Pokemon high in the attack stat that is there to take down the team using strong physical attacks.
  • Rapid Spinner / Spinner: A Pokemon that is there to use rapid spin to remove entry hazards
  • Revenge Killer: A Pokemon whose main purpose on a team is to revenge kill or trap certain threats, usually those who your team lacks a solid defense against. Generally characterized by high Speed and frailty.
  • Special Sweeper: A Pokemon that has a high special attack and uses strong special attack Moves to take down the enemies team.
  • Spin-blocker: A ghost Pokemon who is used to stop the use of rapid spin.
  • Stall-breaker: A Pokemon who is used to break up the opponents strategy.
  • Status Absorber:A Pokemon who can avoid, remove, or use to its advantage one or more status effects.
  • Suicide Lead: A lead Pokemon, who usually pack low defense, that tries to set up entry hazards quickly and prevent opponents from doing the same.
  • Supporter: A Pokemon who uses non-offensive moves which benefit the team.
  • Tank: A Pokemon intended to take either physical or special attacks and hit back, but does not have to do so consistently over the entire course of a match
  • Trapper: A Pokemon intended to corner the opponent and put them into a "lose-lose" situation, where it is dangerous for them to both switch out or stay in and attack
  • Utility Pokemon: A Pokemon who is capable of performing a large variety of tasks, and is usually tailored to one specific task that the team requires. Often, this will be because of a combination of good base stats and a wide move pool.
  • Utility Counter: A defensive Pokemon capable of being tailored to switch in and counter a variety of different threats, but not all at once. Primarily added to a team to counter a specific threat while also dealing with miscellaneous other threats.
  • Wall:A Pokemon intended to take either physical or special attacks extremely effectively and consistently over the course of a battle.
  • Wall-breaker: An offensively oriented Pokemon meant specifically for crushing walls rather than sweeping. This is usually done with extra powerful offensive stats and commonly use both physical and special moves in their move set.

Deciding the roles

When deciding what roles of Pokemon you want to use try to keep your team of six each having a different role and try to match roles to what would work best together like a baton passer and a sweeper would work well since the baton passer can power up a sweeper to be able to deal even more damage to the enemy team. Then another example to two roles that would work well would be a cleric and a stall-breaker since the cleric could help to keep a stall-breaker alive and continue breaking up the enemy's strategy.This is a area that you have to decide what kind of roles you want on your team.
